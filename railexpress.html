Information Sheet

Thank you for your interest in taking part in this study. This information sheet provides details about  the study, why the study is being conducted, what participating would mean for you and how we  will use the information you provide.

What if I have any questions?

What if I have any questions?

You are very welcome to ask any questions you have about this research, at any stage before, during or after  the study. You can contact me, Rayan Siyad (rs2196@york.ac.uk) or my supervisor Dr. Joe Cutting (joe.cutting@york.ac.uk) with any questions  or concerns about this study.

What will happen to your information?

What will happen to your information?

Any personal information, such as name or email, will be kept confidential and stored separately from the data  set. The data will be anonymised, so you will not be directly identifiable from any reports or publications.  In accordance with data protection law, the University of York is the Data Controller for this project. This  means that the University is responsible for making sure your personal information is kept secure,  confidential and anonymous. The University will also ensure that the information is only used in the way you  have been told it will be used. Your information will only be accessible in its original form to me, Rayan Siyad (rs2196@york.ac.uk) and my supervisor Dr. Joe Cutting (joe.cutting@york.ac.uk).


Information from this study will be stored on University of York’s cloud storage systems. The University’s cloud  storage solution is provided by Google, which means that data can be located at any of Google’s globally spread  data centres. The University has data protection compliant arrangements in place with this provider (see https://www.york.ac.uk/it-services/google/policy/privacy/). The University processes personal data for  research purposes under Article 6 (1) (e) of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The data will be  kept until the final marks are formally approved for the module.

 

If you have concerns about how your information is being processed, please contact the University’s Data  Protection Officer at dataprotection@york.ac.uk. If you are concerned about the way in which the University  has handled your personal data, you have a right to lodge a complaint to the Information Commissioner’s Office  (Tel: 0303 123 1113, see www.ico.org.uk/concerns for more information).


Purpose of the study

Purpose of the study

As part of the assessment for my Individual Project: Human-Centred Interactive Technologies,  I am conducting a study to identify user behavior while completing a train booking task.

As a participant you will be given a set of instructions to be followed to complete a train booking task SIMULATION. The instructions will include information to help you complete the booking, such as the departure and arrival destinations, departure and return dates and time, number of passengers, seat preferences etc. On completing the SIMULATION booking you will be asked to complete a questionnaire with questions regarding demographic information and your experience while completing the train booking task.

There are no possible disadvantages in taking part. Unfortunately, I am  unable to offer you any  compensation for your time. However, I am very grateful for your participation and help to complete my assessment.


Your participation

Your participation

You need to be over the age of 18. Your participation is completely voluntary. If you are no longer interested in  taking part, please let me know so I can try to recruit someone else instead. You are free to withdraw at any  time, without having to provide a reason and without any negative consequences. If you wish to withdraw  your data within a period of up to one week after participation, you can do so by contacting me (Rayan Siyad, rs2196@york.ac.uk) or my supervisor (Dr. Joe Cutting, joe.cutting@york.ac.uk).
